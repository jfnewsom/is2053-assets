The Game
Deep in the Heart: A Lone Star Journey
About the Game

Deep in the Heart: A Lone Star Journey is a text-based adventure game inspired by the classic Oregon Trail—but set in modern Texas. Players start in San Antonio and must navigate highways, face random hazards, answer Texas trivia, and hopefully make it to the beach at Corpus Christi or Galveston.

Why text-based? The retro console aesthetic is intentional—it justifies why we’re building this in Python! Oregon Trail didn’t need fancy graphics, and neither does this. The charm is in the writing, the choices, and the dad jokes.

What You’re Building

Over the semester, you’ll build:

  • Module 1: Character creation, destination selection, basic travel
  • Module 2: Loops for continuous travel, random hazards with D20 mechanics
  • Module 3: Functions for modular code, lists for tracking visited cities
  • Module 4: Files for saving/loading, dictionaries for city data
  • Module 5: Classes for professional OOP architecture

How to Play
The Official Game Rules
Win Condition

Reach the beach! The game ends when you arrive at either Corpus Christi or Galveston. There’s no way to lose—the game just keeps going until you make it. Every city in Texas is a valid stop (All My Eggses has restaurants everywhere!), so there are no “bad” choices—just longer routes to the beach.

The Game Loop

The game follows this cycle:

  1. City Phase — Choose Your Destination. You’re in a city. Look at the available routes and pick where you want to go next.
  2. Trivia — Prove You Know Texas. Answer a trivia question about your current city. Correct? Head to your chosen destination. Wrong? GPS malfunction! You’re randomly sent somewhere else.
  3. Travel — Roll the D20. Roll a 20-sided die. Your roll × 10 = miles traveled. Roll a 1? That’s 10 miles. Roll a 20? That’s 200 miles!
  4. Check — Did You Arrive? Yes? If it’s a beach city, you WIN! Otherwise, return to Step 1. No? You haven’t gone far enough. Obstacle time…
  5. Obstacle — Texas Has Other Plans. Face a random Texas hazard—flip a coin! HEADS? You escape! Roll again. TAILS? Lose a turn or go backwards, then roll again.
Movement Rules
  • Route Lock: Once you pick a destination, you’re committed. No changing your mind mid-highway. You must arrive at that city before you can choose a new destination.
  • No Rollover Miles: If you need 30 miles and roll 200, you still just arrive. Extra miles don’t carry over. Every city starts fresh at 0 miles.
  • Backwards Boundaries: If an obstacle sends you backwards, you can only go as far as the nearest city behind you. You can’t leapfrog past cities.
  • City Reset: Whenever you arrive at any city (forwards or backwards), your miles reset to zero and you enter the City Phase with a new trivia question.
What We Track

At the end of the game, you’ll see your trip stats:

Total Miles
Traveled across all legs of the journey
Cities Visited
All stops made on your road trip
Hazards Faced
Total obstacles encountered en route
Hazards Escaped
How many times Texas let you off easy

“What do we not track? Money, fuel, health, time, or inventory. This ain’t that kind of game. Just you, the road, and the beach. Keep it simple.”

How This Course Works
Build → Enhance → Master

Each module follows a three-week sprint cycle. This mirrors how real game studios work—and ensures you never start from scratch:

Week Sprint Goal
Week 1 BUILD Create the foundation. Get the basic feature working.
Week 2 ENHANCE Add complexity. Build on what you made.
Week 3 MASTER Polish and ship. Complete the feature.

The key insight: You’re not doing homework—you’re shipping a product. Each week builds on the last. Each module adds features to the same game. By the end, you’ll have a portfolio piece you can show off.
