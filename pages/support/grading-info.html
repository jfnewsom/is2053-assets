How Your Grade Is Calculated

Your final grade is a weighted average of the four category averages, computed in five steps:

  1. For each category, your lowest score is dropped automatically.
  2. The remaining scores in each category are averaged.
  3. Each category average is multiplied by its weight (25%, 15%, 30%, or 30%).
  4. The four weighted averages are added together — that’s your final percentage.
  5. The percentage is mapped to a letter grade using the Grade Scale below.
calculate
Worked example

Suppose you average 92% on Module Exams (after the lowest is dropped), 88% on Book Exercises, 95% on Foundation Labs, and 90% on Master Labs. Your final grade works out to:

(0.92 × 0.25) + (0.88 × 0.15) + (0.95 × 0.30) + (0.90 × 0.30)

= 0.230 + 0.132 + 0.285 + 0.270 = 0.917 = 91.7%

91.7% falls in the 90.0–91.9 range on the Grade Scale, so the letter grade is A−.

Module Exams

Five Module Exams (one per module) account for 25% of your final grade. They’re auto-graded by Canvas — no manual grading involved. The lowest exam grade is dropped automatically.

Each exam contains multiple choice, true/false, and code tracing. Questions are drawn randomly from a question bank, so each student’s exam is unique.

Rubrics

CodeGrade evaluates each submission against a structured rubric. Each rubric totals 100 points. Categories are scored on a continuous scale, meaning you can earn partial credit anywhere from 0 up to the maximum points listed — not just full points or zero. The Lab rubric applies to all labs (BUILD, ENHANCE, and MASTER) — the categories don’t change, only the specific chapters covered each module.

What counts as on time

Every deadline is 11:59 PM Central Time. Canvas may display deadlines in your local time zone, but the actual cutoff is CT. If you’re working from another time zone, submit early enough to clear the CT deadline.

Why it works this way

This isn’t academic strictness — it’s workplace simulation. In the field, deadlines don’t slide. A software release ships on the planned date or the client launches without it. A sprint ends Friday whether your feature is done or not. A bug fix gets to production before the audit, or the team explains in writing why it didn’t. The professional standard you’ll be measured by once you graduate is binary: did you deliver, or didn’t you?

Think about it from the other side. Does walking into your boss’s office without the work product you were supposed to deliver sound like something you’ll get away with? Maybe once. Twice? Pack your box, you’re done. The companies you’ll work for don’t have a “lowest grade dropped” policy.

I hold you to that same standard here on purpose. If I slid deadlines for “I forgot” or “I had a busy week,” you’d graduate without the muscle memory to meet deadlines that actually matter — and your first job would be where you learned what hard deadlines feel like. That’s not a favor I’m willing to do you. Treat this course like a job, and you’ll do great.

How to plan around it

Submit to CodeGrade early and often before the deadline. Most lab issues surface in the first few submissions, and you have unlimited submissions until the cutoff — use them. Don’t save the work for the last hour. If something genuinely catastrophic happens, your drops are designed for exactly that.

End of term

The final cutoff for the semester is 11:59 PM Tuesday, August 4. Canvas locks at that point — there’s no path to submit anything after the term closes. Plan your final week to wrap everything up before then.

Learn to work this way now, while the consequence is a grade. Once you’re employed, the consequence is your reputation, your contract, or your job.

Grade Visibility & Disputes
Where and when grades are posted

Grades appear in the Canvas gradebook after the submission deadline, typically within 48 hours. CodeGrade results are available immediately upon submission and show the full rubric breakdown — you can see exactly which categories cost you points and resubmit before the deadline.

If you think a grade is wrong

Mistakes happen. If you believe a grade is incorrect, email me within one week of the grade being posted. Include the assignment name and a clear description of what you think was scored incorrectly. After that window, grades are final.
