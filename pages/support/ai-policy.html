AI Policy
ChatGPT, Copilot, and Other AI Tools

AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and GitHub Copilot exist. You’ll probably use them in your career. The question for this course isn’t “can you use AI?” — it’s what role AI plays in your learning.

Here’s the short version, then the longer version explaining why.

You MAY use AI tools to:
  • Explain Python syntax and concepts
  • Troubleshoot errors and decode error messages
  • Understand concepts from the readings
  • Proofread and grammar-check your writing
  • Suggest how to structure or organize code you’ve already written
You MAY NOT use AI tools to:
  • Generate code you submit as your own work
  • Answer quiz questions directly
  • Fabricate output or screenshots
  • Complete BookEx exercises on your behalf
  • Do your actual hands-on coding
Guiding Principle
Tutor, Not Ghostwriter

AI can help you understand and improve your work, but the work itself must be yours. Think of AI like a tutor, not a ghostwriter.

A tutor explains concepts, points you toward the right approach, helps you debug your thinking, and asks questions back. A ghostwriter does the work for you and hands you a product. The first builds your skills; the second hides what you don’t know.

Why This Policy Exists
The Longer Version
AI multiplies what you already know. It doesn’t replace what you don’t.

If you understand the topic, AI accelerates you. If you don’t, AI gives you confident-sounding output you can’t evaluate, defend, or fix when it’s wrong. Zero times anything is still zero.

A student who can’t explain what a for loop does will not become a programmer by asking ChatGPT to write the lab. They’ll just have code they can’t defend on a quiz, in office hours when I ask “walk me through line 14,” or in a job interview when someone asks them to modify it.

The most transferable skill you’ll build this semester is learning how to ask better questions.

The students who get the most out of AI tools are the ones who already have the vocabulary to ask precisely. “Fix my code” is a beginner prompt. “This Python script throws a KeyError on line 14 when the input dict is missing the ‘status’ field — should I use .get() with a default, or catch the exception?” is a professional prompt. The difference isn’t the AI — it’s the human knowing enough domain vocabulary to frame the problem.

That’s why the readings, the BookEx exercises, and the hands-on lab work matter. They build the vocabulary that makes AI actually useful to you. Skip the work, skip the vocabulary, and AI becomes a crutch that hides what you don’t know — right up until the moment someone asks you to explain it.

Validate everything.

AI confidently produces wrong answers. Hallucinated method names, deprecated syntax, wrong indentation, code that imports modules that don’t exist, examples that look right but throw runtime errors. If you can’t independently verify what it gave you — by running it, reading the official Python docs, or checking against the textbook — you can’t use it. “The AI told me” is not a defense. In a professional setting, your name goes on the deliverable, not the model’s.

Scope Compliance
How We Detect AI-Generated Code

CodeGrade uses Scope Compliance checking. It automatically detects if your code uses techniques we haven’t covered yet.

AI-generated code almost always uses advanced features, libraries, or patterns that students in an intro course wouldn’t know yet. When we see list comprehensions in Module 1, or try/except in Module 2, or regex in Module 3… we know.

error
Consequence

Scope violations result in an automatic zero. Using techniques you haven’t learned is clear evidence of outside help.

The Honest Test
Walk Me Through It

If I asked you to walk me through the code you submitted, line by line, could you do it without your AI tab open?

If the answer is no, the work isn’t yours yet. Go back and learn it before you submit.

The goal of this course is to make you a programmer, not to make you good at prompting AI. You’ll have plenty of time to use AI tools later — but first, you need to understand what the AI is actually doing. Build the foundation now. AI will be more powerful in your hands for the rest of your career if you do.
