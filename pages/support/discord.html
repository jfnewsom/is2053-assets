Server Rules
Expectations & Guidelines
Rule 1 — Protect Your Privacy

Use a nickname or first name only. Do not share your student ID, full name, phone number, address, or other personally identifiable information in any channel.

Rule 2 — Keep Academic Records Off Discord

Do not post screenshots of grades, Canvas notifications, or instructor feedback. These conversations happen in email or office hours, not in a public server.

Rule 3 — Ask in Channels, Not DMs

Questions asked publicly help everyone. If you have a question, others probably do too. Posting in #help-python means your classmates can benefit from the answer as well.

Rule 4 — Be Respectful. Zero Tolerance for Toxicity.

This is a professional learning community. Treat your instructor, classmates, and alumni with respect at all times.

  • No personal attacks, insults, harassment, or bullying
  • No passive-aggressive comments or hostile tone
  • No public venting about grades or deadlines—take it to email
  • Disagreements happen; handle them like a professional or walk away
Rule 5 — No Academic Integrity Violations

Helping each other learn is encouraged. Sharing quiz answers, copying lab work, or completing assignments for others is not. When in doubt, help someone understand the concept—don’t hand them the answer.

error
Enforcement

Minor rule violations may receive a warning. Toxic, hostile, or disrespectful behavior results in immediate permanent removal without warning. Harassment, threats, or academic integrity violations will also be reported to UT San Antonio Student Conduct.

Server Channels
Where to Post

The server is organized into channels so conversations stay focused and easy to search. Here’s where everything lives:

#introductions
Say hi when you join! Tell us your name, major, and one thing you hope to build with Python.
#general
Off-topic chat, wins, interesting finds, and community conversation.
#help-python
Code questions and debugging. Use code blocks when pasting error messages or code snippets.
#module-channels
Module-specific discussions—one channel per module for focused conversation about that week’s content.
#announcements
Read-only. Important course updates, reminders, and links. Check here if something feels off.
#resources
Useful links, tools, tutorials, and references shared by students and the instructor.
