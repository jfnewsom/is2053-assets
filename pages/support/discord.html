This course has a dedicated Discord server for collaboration, peer support, and community. Participation is entirely optional—no course content or grading depends on it. Before joining, you must read and understand the following terms.

What Discord Is For
✓ Quick Q&A and peer-to-peer help
✓ Lab troubleshooting with classmates
✓ Networking with students and alumni
✓ Informal discussion about coding
✓ Community building and mentorship
What Discord Is NOT For
✗ Assignment submissions (Canvas only)
✗ Grade discussions or appeals
✗ Deadline extensions or accommodations
✗ Private academic matters
✗ Anything that belongs in an email
⚠️ Read This Carefully

This Discord server is not operated, managed, or monitored by The University of Texas at San Antonio. It is a third-party platform subject to Discord's own Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, not UT San Antonio's data protection policies.

UT San Antonio cannot guarantee the security, privacy, or availability of any content shared on this server. Your participation is entirely voluntary and at your own discretion.

Expectations & Guidelines
1. Protect Your Privacy. Use a nickname or first name only. Do not share your student ID, full name, phone number, address, or other personally identifiable information.
2. Keep Academic Records Off Discord. Do not post screenshots of grades, Canvas notifications, or instructor feedback. These conversations happen in email or office hours.
3. Ask in Channels, Not DMs. Questions asked publicly help everyone. If you have a question, others probably do too.
4. Be Respectful—Zero Tolerance for Toxicity. This is a professional learning community. Treat your instructor, TAs, classmates, and alumni with respect at all times.
  • No personal attacks, insults, harassment, or bullying
  • No passive-aggressive comments or hostile tone
  • No public venting about grades or deadlines—take it to email
  • Disagreements happen; handle them like a professional or walk away
5. No Academic Integrity Violations. Helping each other learn is encouraged. Sharing quiz answers, copying lab work, or completing assignments for others is not.

Enforcement: Minor rule violations may receive a warning. Toxic, hostile, or disrespectful behavior results in immediate permanent removal without warning. Harassment, threats, or academic integrity violations will also be reported to UT San Antonio Student Conduct.
