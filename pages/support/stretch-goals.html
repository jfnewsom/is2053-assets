Stretch Goals
Extra Credit Through Codewars Challenges

Codewars is a platform where you solve coding challenges (called “kata”) to sharpen your skills. For this course, completing kata earns you extra credit points toward your final grade.

This is 100% optional. It’s for students who want extra practice or need to boost their grade.

Platform
codewars.com — free account, no payment required
Language
Python only — other languages do not count toward this course’s extra credit
Max Extra Credit
Up to +5% added to your final grade
Deadline
Last day of classes — no late submissions accepted
How It Works
Getting Started with Codewars
Steps to Earn Extra Credit
  1. Create a free account at codewars.com
  2. Choose Python as your language
  3. Solve kata at 8 kyu, 7 kyu, or 6 kyu difficulty (easiest to harder)
  4. Take a screenshot showing your completed kata and your honor points total
  5. Submit the screenshot to the Stretch Goals assignment in Canvas
lightbulb
Where to Start

Start with 8 kyu kata and search for tags like “Fundamentals,” “Strings,” “Basic Language Features,” or “Mathematics.” These align well with what we’re covering in class and are a great way to reinforce what you’re learning each week.

Honor Thresholds
Extra Credit Based on Honor Points

Extra credit is based on your total Honor points earned on Codewars. Honor accumulates as you complete kata—higher honor means more extra credit.

Honor Points Extra Credit Approximate Effort
25+ Honor +1% ~10 easy kata
50+ Honor +2% ~20 easy kata
100+ Honor +3% ~35–40 kata
200+ Honor +4% Dedicated practice
300+ Honor +5% (MAX) Serious commitment!
Rules
Requirements for Extra Credit
What Counts
  • Only Python solutions count (no JavaScript, Ruby, or other languages)
  • Only kata at 8 kyu, 7 kyu, or 6 kyu difficulty (harder kata are beyond course scope)
  • You must submit by the last day of classes—no late submissions
  • Screenshot must show your username, honor total, and completed kata list
error
Do NOT Use AI to Solve Kata

The entire point of Stretch Goals is to build real problem-solving skill through practice. Using AI to generate solutions defeats that purpose entirely and won’t help you on exams or in future courses. Solve them yourself—that’s where the growth happens.
