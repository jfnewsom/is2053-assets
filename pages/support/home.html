This course is structured as a semester-long game development engagement.

You’re a Junior Developer at Bat City Collective, an Austin indie game studio. Over five modules you’ll build a retro text adventure called Deep in the Heart: A Lone Star Journey for your client — the Texas-themed restaurant chain All My Eggses Live in Texas. Every module is a sprint: BUILD a feature, ENHANCE it, then MASTER it.

This isn’t homework. It’s a product. The game has a real brief, real constraints, real stakeholders, and a real shipping deadline at the end of the term. The Python concepts you learn — variables, loops, functions, files, classes — all serve the game. Learn more about the scenario or explore the Texas highway map you’ll navigate.

The Engagement
Your Studio
Bat City Collective

Austin indie game studio specializing in retro-style games and text adventures. Founded by devs burned out by AAA crunch culture. Five mentors will guide you across the project: Danny (Lead Dev), Priya (PM), Sasha (Creative), T-Bone (QA), and Tyler (Junior Dev, your peer).

Your Client
All My Eggses Live in Texas

Texas-themed family restaurant chain (think Cracker Barrel meets Buc-ee’s). 47 locations. They want a retro game for the tablets that are replacing the peg games on every table. The brief: “Oregon Trail but TEXAS. Family-friendly. 5–10 minutes. No violence.”

Module Structure: BUILD → ENHANCE → MASTER

Each module follows three lab stages. BUILD (Lab X.1) introduces a new feature in your game. ENHANCE (Lab X.2) extends it. MASTER (Lab X.3) polishes and ships it. Carry forward your code from each stage to the next. See Assignment Overview for details. Module 5 runs BUILD + MASTER only (no ENHANCE) since Ch11 is dropped.
